# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

College students navigating a multi-semester degree plan across disjoint tools (Blackboard, calendars, email, grade portals). They are overwhelmed by fragmentation and want a single connected view of their academic progress — courses, grades, deadlines, and prereqs — that persists across semesters instead of resetting every term.

## Product Purpose

Synapse turns a degree plan into one connected academic graph. Every course is a node with topic notes and prereq edges. Every grade is a weighted event. Every semester is a container. This structure makes cross-semester queries possible: "what happens to my capstone if I take Algorithms a semester early?"

The landing page's job is to make this connectedness viscerally obvious before the user signs up. The product itself will expand into an app later.

## Brand Personality

**Warm, sharp, grounded.** Approachable but precise. Paper-and-pencil warmth meets systems thinking. Should feel like a well-organized binder of notes — physically grounded, but with the rigor of a dependency graph underneath.

## Anti-references

- No SaaS clichés: no hero-metric stats (big number + small label), no glassmorphism, no gradient text, no testimonials carousel
- No corporate/template feel: should not look like a generic startup landing page template on a beige background

## Design Principles

1. **Practice what you preach** — The landing page itself should demonstrate connectedness. The hero graph is the centerpiece, not decoration. The page's own structure (loose tools → connected graph → extracted data) mirrors the product's value.
2. **Warmth without sentimentality** — Paper texture, warm neutrals, green ink. The warmth comes from groundedness (paper, pen, real course names) not from cozy copy or emoji. The green acts as the signal of "connected" (present in the graph) against the warm paper.
3. **Show, don't tell** — Every claim about the product has a visual demonstration. The "six tools" section shows the problem (scattered), the graph section shows the solution (connected), the syllabus section shows the mechanism (extraction).
4. **Expert confidence** — Trust the student to understand the complexity of their own degree. No patronizing simplification. The dependency graph with real course codes, the week-by-week timeline, the what-if grade projection — all signal that this is a serious tool for people who take their education seriously.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.2 AA. Adequate contrast throughout, reduced-motion support for all animations, screen-reader-friendly labels on the graph and visual sections.
